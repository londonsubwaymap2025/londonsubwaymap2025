A Historical Journey Through the Campus:

QMUL’s campus is a tapestry woven from the history of several institutions, each contributing its unique architectural character and legacy. The main campus, located in Mile End, is primarily centered around the historic buildings of the former Queen Mary College. Founded in 1887 as the East London Technical College, the institution evolved into Queen Mary College, later merging with the University of London in 1989 to form QMUL.

The campus reflects this evolution, featuring a blend of Victorian and modern architecture. The iconic Queen Mary Building, a grand Victorian structure, serves as a prominent landmark, its clock tower a symbol of the university’s rich history. Other notable buildings include the elegant Charterhouse Square building, the contemporary Sir William Henry Bragg Building, and the modern, state-of-the-art Mile End campus, showcasing QMUL’s commitment to both tradition and innovation.
